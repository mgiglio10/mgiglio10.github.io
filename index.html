Why I made this

After learning about solids of revolution in my calculus class, I became interested in creating a program that could find the volume of a solid given user-inputed criteria. While the program is capable of finding the volume of horizontally and vertically revolved solids, the goal is to expand functionality to solids around slant lines.


How it works

To avoid the issue of finding an inverse function when finding the volume of a vertically revolved solid, I used Pappus' Theorem (V = Ad, where A is the area of the enclosed region and d is the distance travelled by the centroid). I then created a numerical integral calculator so I could find the area of the region and the x coordinate of the centroid. For horizontally revolved solids, I simply used the integral calculator to use the disk method of finding volume.
